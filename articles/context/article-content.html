Context

A programmer's approach to understanding the election.

Frameworks

Often, in order to find the best way forward, we programmers have to let go of whatever mental framework we've built up in an attempt to better understand a problem.

In order to understand this election, I've had to cast off my own mental framework for politics in the US. And I think I'm better for it. I want to share that with you, because I do believe some good will come of this result.

Before I continue, I want to acknowledge and point out some things.

  • I am a heterosexual, cis, white, upper-middle-class male.
  • I am Canadian and know less than five rural Americans.
  • I am moving to New York City in three days.

What I'm saying is I don't know what I'm talking about. I have not felt the pain of having my entire race or gender insulted by a president-elect. I hardly know anything about US culture. The degree to which this election affects me is probably very small.

But I'm a programmer, and that means I take a very measured approach to learning and setting myself up for new information. Here's how I'm setting myself up to understand this.

Opposition

No matter who you support, this is what you've been told.

  • The other candidate is the second coming of Hitler
  • The other candidate's party has been in power during all of America's worst moments
  • The other candidate has openly and proudly insulted millions of Americans
  • There's something about the other candidate's mind that makes him or her categorically unfit for the presidency
  • It is okay to absolutely detest the other candidate

The list continues. Think about the opposition - how many of those points do you actually believe? In my case, all of them. Deep down, reading those things and thinking about Trump fills me with a deep, resounding agreement. Of COURSE those things are true. It's so obvious! Trumps supporters must be blind, or brainwashed, or both, if they can't see it!

But those are my emotions talking. As a programmer, I've got some practice putting those aside and taking a more pragmatic view of things.

Spectrum

My framework of the election used to look something like this:

  • Trump supporters must be absolute bigots because they don't care that he expresses bigotry
  • Trump supporters must be foolish because Trump will clearly put the country in jeopardy as president
  • Trump supporters must be heartless because the don't care about his insults to the rest of the country

There are issues here. All of these points are very absolute, and have me imagining Trump's supporters as a single, very unpleasant human being, multiplied by 59,588,434. Sometimes the framework has to be adjusted to allow some flexibility.

I wrote this Nov 9th, 2016. I was in Montreal.
