Primer: What's MIDI?

MIDI, which stands for Musical Instrument Digital Interface, is a protocol for communication between digital instruments and things that make sounds.

You can think of it as a way for your digital piano to talk to your computer: when you play a note on your piano keyboard, your computer is told which note was played, and how hard it was pressed. Your computer can then use this information to make a noise. Perhaps a piano noise, but perhaps a guitar, a drum, a synthesizer, or anything else your music-making computer might have installed.
